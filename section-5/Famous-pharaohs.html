Famous Pharaohs

Tutankhamun

Reign: c. 1332–1323 BCE (New Kingdom) Dynasty: 18th Dynasty Born: c. 1341 BCE Died: c. 1323 BCE, aged 18

He ascended to the throne at a young age of around 8 or 9 due to the early death of his father Akhenaten, who is famous for trying to establish the worship of the sun god Aton.

He is one of the most famous pharaohs due to the intact tomb of his that was found by the archeologist Howard Carter, in 1922. His reign was shortlived but his tomb contained incredible amount of wealth.

Ramesses II (Ramesses the Great)

Reign: c. 1279–1213 BCE (New Kingdom) Dynasty: 19th Dynasty Born: c. 1303 BCE Died: 1213 BCE, aged 90

He was one of the most famous Pharaohs and the most successful and celebrated. He was considered one of the best pharaohs due to the immense development he was able to bring . During his reign, Egypt had reached the peak of its military, economic, and cultural power. He had fought some great battles like the battle of Kadesh (c. 1274 BCE), fought against the Hittites. The military campaigns helped secure the borders of the country and maintain its influence around the neighbouring kingdoms and territories as well.

Cleopatra VII

Reign: 51–30 BCE (Ptolemaic Dynasty) Born: 69 BCE Died: 30 BCE, aged 39

Cleopatra VII was the last ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt, eventhough she was greek heritage, she was accepted as a queen as she embraced the culture of egypt . She became a queen at the age of 18 after the death of her father , she intitally had shared the reign with her brother but due to various conflicts she faought to keep her position and was successful in doing so. A major reason for the win over the kingdom against her brother was her alliance with Julius Caeser, who helped her in reclaiming the throne. Cleopatra's cause of death was suicide which she had commited after loosing the Battle of Actium in 31 BCE
