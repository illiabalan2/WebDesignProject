History of Egypt

Early Dynastic Period

The Early Dynastic Period (c. 3100–2686 BCE) marked the formation of a unified Egypt under the first pharaohs. It was a time of consolidation and state-building. It had the first dynasty of the Pharaohs, who had layed the foundation for becoming the one of most powerful and influential civilizations in the world.

King Narmer is known to be one of the first to be credited for unifying the upper and lower Egypt which had previously been divided due to various reasons like geographical differences, cultural & political issues, pre-dynastic rivalry etc.

Writing had been developed, a centralized government had been layed . During that time the capital city Memphis was the political and religious center which had various temples located.

Old Kingdom

The Old Kingdom (c. 2686–2181 BCE) is known as the "Age of the Pyramids," with the construction of the Great Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx.. Monarchy was well established and the pharaoh was considered to be of great importance both politically and religiously, he was regarded as a living god. He was often compared to the Horus, who was the falcon-headed god of warfare.

As the power was centralized, roles were assigned strategically. The bureaucracy had been organized well. The kingdom had provinces, often known as nomes and the regional governer who ruled over the nomach was called the nomarch.. There was also the pharaohs chief official the Vizier

The great pyramid of Giza , which was built for Pharaoh Khufu still remains to be one of the seven wonders of the world. This pyramid includes the sphinx, which is a statue with the body of the lion and the head of the pharaoh.

The Step Pyramid of Djoser is an example of great architecture as it was leap from the older design of flat tops on the pyramids.

Middle Kingdom

The Middle Kingdom (c. 2050–1710 BCE) saw the rise of the 12th dynasty, the establishment of a golden age of art, literature, and culture.It was seen as the time of recovery.Mentuhotep II of the 11th dynasty(c. 2134–1991 BCE)is regarded as the founder of the middle kingdom , He defeated the rivals and had restablished the rule of the pharaohs. Amenemhat I of the 12th Dynasty(c. 1991–1802 BCE) had further made great improvements and and reforms, in military strategy, administration etc.

There was a rise of the Middle Kingdom which was known as their golden age which was from Senusret I (c. 1971–1926 BCE) was known for his military campaigns in Nubia, which led to the expansion of the borders of Egypt.

Agriculture was considered to be the main source of economy for Egypt and during the time of the Middle Kingdom the agricultural industry had flourished dur to the vast infrastructural improvements like the Nile Delta and in Fayoum. Even the Trade with the neighbouring regions Levant, Nubia flourished.

New Kingdom

The New Kingdom 1550 BCE to 1070 BCE saw the rise of the 18th dynasty, under Pharaoh Ahmose I who succeded in expelling Hyksos.The pharaohs of the New Kingdom expanded Egypt's borders, making it an empire.It was marked by marked by military conquests, monumental architecture, cultural achievements.

Late Period

The Late Period of ancient Egypt is time between 664 BCE and 332 BCE, covering the 26th Dynasty from the conquest of Egypt by Alexander the Great.The 26th Dynasty is also known as the Saite Dynasty.Psamtik I (c. 664–610 BCE) is one of the most notable rulers of this dynasty. Psamtik I established a strong central government and helped in reviving the military. In the 525 BCE the persian king had invaded egypt ,this marked the Persian domination in the period from (c.525 BCE - 332 BCE) until it was then conquered by Alexander the Great. This period was also marked with the decline of political power,followed by the foreign infuence and invasions.
