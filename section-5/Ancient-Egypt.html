The Pyramids and Monuments of Egypt

The Great Pyramids of Giza

It is also known as the Pyramid of Khufu . It is the largest pyramid out of the three that are present at the Giza plateau. It was contructed by the Fourth Dynasty at around 2580–2560 BCE. The pyramid was built as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu

Key facts

The Great Pyramid stands at 146.6 meters (481 feet) tall and it was the tasllest man-made structure for 3,800 years. It has been constructed using 2.3 million limestone blocks, with each block weighing at about 2.5 to 15 tons each and each of these blocks has been placed with great precision.

Construction Theories:

There are a lot of theories on the construction of such a wonder. Ramps of various different types like straight or spiral were used to transport each limestone block. The contrusction is said to have required a massive workforce of 20,000 to 30,000 laborers who worked in shifts. The alignment of the pyramid has been done with the help of cardinal directions and the precise dimensions sugggest that some kind of an advanced tool may have been used for the purpose of measurements.

The valley of the Kings

It is one of the most famous archeological sites of Egypt , located by the west of the river Nile near the city Luxor. It is the burial site for Egypts pharaohs, queens, nobles from the 18th to the 20th Dynasty. The tombs present are in the shape of cliffs. Some of the notable tombs in the valley of kings are the Tomb of Tutankhamun, Tomb of Ramses II,Tomb of Seti I . The tombs were designed is such a way as to elude robbers, It has narrow passageways and hidden entrances.

The Temples of Karnak and Luxor

The Karnak Temple Complex is a vast open-air museum and one of the largest ancient religious sites in the world. The Luxor Temple stands as an incredible monument to Egypt’s pharaohs. The Karnak Temple Complex is a complex comprising of buildings that were dedicated to Amun, Mut who was his wife and the son Khonsu.The construction had begun around 2000 BCE but the rest of the bulk structures were built during the New Kingdom(c. 1550–1070 BCE) under the pharaohs Seti I and Ramses II.

It has a Great Hypostyle Hall: This is perhaps the most famous part of Karnak which is a hall covering a distance of 50,000 sq feet, in an area with 134 columns.

The Karnak Temple also has the Obelisk of Hatsheput and the Colossi of Memnon which are the statues of pharaohs.

The Temple of Luxor is dedicated to a group of gods, but it was dedicated to the Theban Triad but it was specifically dedicated to Amun-Ra, who was the king of the gods and also associated with the pharaohs that ruled over egypt.
