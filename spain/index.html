Spain

In this section you will dive into some of the architectural wonders of Spain that have an incredible history and legacy

La Sagrada Família, Barcelona

La Sagrada Familia, located in the heart of Barcelona, Spain, is an architectural marvel that blends Gothic and Art Nouveau styles. Designed by the renowned Catalan architect Antoni Gaudí, this UNESCO World Heritage Site has captivated visitors from around the world for over a century. Its intricate design and symbolic significance make it one of the most visited landmarks in Europe.
Read More

Alhambra Palace, Granada

Alhambra Palace is a breathtaking blend of Islamic art and architecture, the Alhambra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that crowns the hills of Granada. Explore the intricate Nasrid Palaces, serene Generalife Gardens, and the historic Alcazaba, all surrounded by the beauty of the Sierra Nevada.
Read More

Roman Theatre, Cartagena

Cartagena's Roman Theater is a magnificent 1st-century amphitheater rediscovered after centuries of obscurity. With its grand seating and immersive museum, this iconic site offers a glimpse into the cultural life of ancient Carthago Nova.
Read More

Tower of Hercules, La Coruña

The world’s oldest functioning lighthouse, the Tower of Hercules is a marvel of Roman engineering and a symbol of Galicia. Standing tall against the Atlantic, this UNESCO landmark combines ancient history, breathtaking views, and legendary tales of Hercules himself.
